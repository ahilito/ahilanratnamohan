Invasive Language

language-learning monoloog | english as language and theme| words as plants | words as migrants | horticultural research



This project is an attempt at archiving language.  Through an intimate monologue, I trace the shifts in my own language: the gradual deterioration of my English during my time living in Belgium, the neutralisation of my Australian accent, and my daughters’ increasingly Americanised accents. These lived experiences sit at the heart of the work.


In 'Invasive Language' I draw parallels between words and plants, examining how my linguistic journey intersects with concepts around native, non-native and invasive plants. At the core oo the work is the curry leaf plant, karuvpilai in Tamil, and its significance in the Tamil community; a plant which survived the physical migration, but did not manage to migrate linguistically.


As the text develops, I slowly introduce the different linguistic phenomena which have come to inhabit my psyche during this period; the Flemish sentence structures which return in my youngest daughter’s English, the Dutch words which I can’t avoid, the use of double-adjectives and adverbs (eg. fast-fast) from Tamil.  And finally I attempt to re-discover the Australian accent, which I subconsciously unlearned in order to make myself more comprehendible in Europe.  In doing so I look at the migrant’s language experience in Australia, the imperialised nature of English, the neutralisation of my Australian accent and my daughters’ increasingly Americanised accents. What does an Australian accent mean within the global hierarchies of linguistic prestige?
